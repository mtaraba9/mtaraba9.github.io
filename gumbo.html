Chicken and Sausage Gumbo

My mother is from New Orleans, I love gumbo

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup of white flour
  • 3/4 cup of vegetable oil
  • 2 sticks of celery, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 white onion, chopped
  • Pinch of file powder
  • 3 teaspoons cayenne pepper
  • 3 teaspoons cajun salt
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 lb of jalepeno andoile sausage
  • 2 bay leafs
  • 6 cups of chicken broth
  • 1 rotisserie chicken, shredded and de-boned

Instructions

  1. Once the roux has a nice light brown color turn the stove to medium and add the celery, bell pepper, and white onion. Stir all of the ingredients until the roux has coated all of the vegetables. Add the file powder, cayanne pepper, and cajun salt and stir the mixture. Keep stirring for 4 minutes Add garlic and sausage and cook for three more minutes.
  2. Heat up dutch oven or large pot to medium-high heat. Add vegetable oil and flour. Stir continuously for 10-15 minutes until mixture is the color of chocolate milk. This mixture is known as a roux. If the flour burns you will see dark black specks. If the roux burns, start over. Be careful, the roux becomes very hot and if it gets on you it will burn you. Use a wooden spoon, plastic will melt.
  3. Add the chicken broth and bay leaves. Reduce heat to low and cook for 1 hour.
  4. Add shredded chicken and cook for another 40 minutes until gumbo reaches desired thickness.
  5. Serve over rice.
